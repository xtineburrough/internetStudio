Assignment 1 Guide

Press each button to reveal the steps to completing the first project.

Begin by completing all of Chapters 1 and 2. After you create the "Think Different" example you are ready to choose and design your own manifesto-turned-concrete-poem.
Revisit Module 2.1 and choose the manifesto that you want to design. You do not have to design the complete text, especially if it is long. You may choose to work with a line of text or 8 lines of text. Keep the scope within reason. Your design will be published in one web page.
Sketch the concrete poem on paper. Keep your sketch loose. Use rectangles as stand-ins for letters.
When you are ready to code your project I strongly suggest creating a new file named "manifesto.html" within your view-source repo. If you want to create a new repo (some students have made a manifesto repo, for instance), be sure to give the repo a one-word name (like "manifesto") or use dashes or underscores. Do not put spaces in the name of your repo or your files. If you make a new repo, you must remember to turn on GitHub Pages from the settings. Once you determine where you will save your file on GitHub.com, edit the piece in GitHub Codespaces.
Stage and Commit your changes in Codespaces, then return to your GitHub.com repository. This is the big moment. Make sure you have enabled GitHub Pages. Take a look at your Deployments. The live version of your page will be at http://yourGithubUsername.io/theNameOfTheRepoWhereYouSavedYourWork/theFileNameOfYourManifesto.html

For instance, if your username is janie and you saved a file called "manifesto.html" inside your view-source repo, your live page would be found at https://janie.github.io/view-source/manifesto.hml
